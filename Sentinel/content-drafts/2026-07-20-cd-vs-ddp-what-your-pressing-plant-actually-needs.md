**HEAVY MUSIC MASTERING FOR PRESSING PLANTS**
==============================================

As a mastering engineer with 5000+ credits since 2008, I, Jack Control, work with clients who strive to produce high-quality physical releases. When preparing your heavy music for pressing, it's essential to understand the difference between CD and DDP (Disc Description Protocol) formats. This guide will walk you through what your pressing plant actually needs.

## What is CD Format?
---------------------

A CD (Compact Disc) is a standard format for digital audio, typically mastered at 16-bit, 44.1 kHz. While some pressing plants may still accept CD format, it's essential to note that DDP is the preferred format for high-end physical releases.

## What is DDP Format?
---------------------

DDP (Disc Description Protocol) is a format specifically designed for mastering and replicating physical media. It's a more advanced format than CD, capable of storing metadata and image information. DDP is widely accepted by pressing plants and is the preferred format for heavy music releases.

### DDP Specifications

* **Resolution**: 24-bit, 44.1 kHz (or higher)
* **Format**: ISO 9660
* **File Size**: Typically 500-700 MB
* **Metadata**: Includes track information, album art, and other relevant data

## What Does Your Pressing Plant Actually Need?
------------------------------------------------

To ensure smooth delivery of your heavy music to the pressing plant, you'll need to provide the following:

* **DDP File**: A single file containing the masters, metadata, and image information
* **WAV Files**: Optional, but recommended for high-end releases
* **Image Files**: High-resolution artwork for the album and inserts

### Recommended DDP Software

* **Maestro**: A popular DDP software for mastering and preparing physical media
* **Discmaker**: A professional-grade DDP software for high-end releases
