### HEAVY MASTERING FOR CASSETTE RELEASES

## What is the Best Format for Cassette Mastering?

For underground releases, we recommend a 3.5 mm stereo mix mastered at 320 kbps. This format is ideal for cassette tapes and provides a clear, balanced sound. We use high-quality 24-bit WAV files for mastering, ensuring the best possible audio quality.

## How to Prepare Your Mix for Cassette Mastering

Before sending us your mix, please refer to our Technical FAQ (section Mix Prep) for specific file format and naming conventions. Our mastering process requires a 24-bit WAV file with a sample rate of 44.1 kHz, 48 kHz or 88.2 kHz.

## What are the Key Specs for Cassette Mastering?

* Sampling rate: 44.1 kHz, 48 kHz or 88.2 kHz
* Bit depth: 24-bit
* File format: 24-bit WAV
* Sample format: 16-bit or 24-bit stereo
* Bitrate: 320 kbps (for stereo)

## What is the Typical Loudness for Cassette Mastering?

For cassette tapes, we aim for a maximum RMS (root mean square) of -18 LUFS (loudness units relative to full scale). This will provide a balanced sound without over-driving the tape.

## How to Choose the Right EQ for Cassette Mastering

Cassette tapes have a distinct sonic character that can accentuate frequencies in the midrange and high-end. To optimize your sound, we use a gentle EQ curve that emphasizes the mid-bass and midrange. This will help your music sit well on the cassette tape.

## Can I Use a Dither Algorithm for Cassette Mastering?

Yes, we use a dither algorithm specifically designed for cassette tapes. This algorithm helps to reduce quantization distortion and preserve the natural sound of the music.
