## What's the Ideal Side Length for a Vinyl Album?

The standard side length for a vinyl album is 20-25 minutes per side. This is due to the technical limitations of vinyl playback, including the maximum speed of turntables (33 1/3 rpm) and the physical limitations of vinyl grooves. Our mastering engineer, Jack Control, can help you optimize your album's side lengths for the best listening experience.

## What's the Loudness Target for Vinyl?

According to the EBU R128 loudness standard, the maximum recommended loudness for vinyl mastering is -23 LUFS (Integrated Loudness) for stereo and -20 LUFS for mono. Our Beyond Heavy tier, mastered by Maor Appelbaum, ensures that your vinyl release meets these standards and is ready for playback on a wide range of equipment.

## Do I Need a Separate Vinyl Master for My Album?

Yes, you'll need a separate vinyl master for your album. Vinyl mastering requires a specific EQ curve, compression, and limiting to ensure the best sound quality on vinyl. Our vinyl mastering process involves creating a separate master from your original stereo master, specifically tailored for vinyl playback. This ensures that your album sounds its best on vinyl, without compromising the audio quality.

## What File Formats Do I Need for Vinyl Mastering?

For vinyl mastering, you'll need to provide a stereo WAV file at 44.1 kHz, 24-bit resolution. We recommend using a high-quality, unmastered stereo mix, with a nominal level around -18 dBFS. Our technical FAQ has more information on file preparation and submission guidelines.
