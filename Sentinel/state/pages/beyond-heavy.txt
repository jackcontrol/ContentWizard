BEYOND HEAVY FAQ What is Beyond Heavy? Beyond Heavy is Enormous Door’s premium collaborative mastering service with Jack Control and Maor Appelbaum. It is built for release-ready heavy records that need extra weight, depth, polish, translation, and format-conscious mastering for serious releases. How is Beyond Heavy different from Heavy Mastering? Heavy Mastering is the core Enormous Door mastering service. Beyond Heavy is the flagship collaboration: Jack Control shapes the mastering direction, impact, genre feel, translation, sequencing, and release-format needs, while Maor Appelbaum adds his elite mastering chain, second perspective, and high-end finishing power. Who is Beyond Heavy for? Beyond Heavy is for serious heavy bands, labels, and release-ready projects where the mix is already strong and the final master needs premium treatment across vinyl, streaming, CD, cassette, or digital formats. Does Beyond Heavy include vinyl and streaming masters? Beyond Heavy is designed for projects that need required release formats, including vinyl, streaming, CD, cassette, and digital delivery when applicable. Exact deliverables are confirmed when the project is submitted. Do I need a finished mix before starting? Yes. Beyond Heavy is for finished, release-ready mixes. Your mix should be high-resolution, balanced, punchy, emotionally defined, and not crushed by unnecessary limiting. What if my mix is not ready? If your mix is not ready, fix the mix before mastering. Use the Technical FAQ and Mix Prep section for final file-prep guidance before uploading. Recording Roadmap is for broader production planning, not final mastering file preparation. How much does Beyond Heavy cost? Beyond Heavy starts at $650 for EP-length projects and scales by total project length. Current pricing is listed on the Pricing page. Is Beyond Heavy just about making the record louder? No. Loudness is only one part of mastering. Beyond Heavy is about impact, depth, translation, tone, sequencing, release-format preparation, and preserving the danger and identity of the record while making it feel finished.
